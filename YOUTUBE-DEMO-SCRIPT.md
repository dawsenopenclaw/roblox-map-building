# ForjeGames — YouTube Demo Script
**Title:** AI Builds My Roblox Game In 2 Minutes
**Thumbnail text:** AI BUILDS MY ROBLOX GAME
**Duration:** 2:00
**Target:** Roblox developers, ages 13-18

---

## [0:00 – 0:10] HOOK

**SCREEN:** Black screen, then slow zoom into a blank Roblox Studio viewport. Nothing in it. Just grey grid.

**NARRATOR:**
> "What if you never had to touch a single brick to build a Roblox game? No scripting. No terrain tools. No spending six hours on a castle you hate. Bro, watch this."

---

## [0:10 – 0:22] OPEN THE EDITOR

**SCREEN:** Browser opens to forjegames.com. The dark UI loads — gold accents, clean chat interface. Cursor moves to the prompt bar.

**NARRATOR:**
> "This is ForjeGames. It's an AI that builds complete Roblox games from a description. I'm just going to type what I want."

---

## [0:22 – 0:38] FIRST PROMPT

**SCREEN:** Cursor clicks the chat input. Hands visible on keyboard. Type in real time:

> `build me a medieval castle with a moat`

Hit Enter. The AI response streams in — code blocks, explanations, asset IDs. Scrolling output. Fast.

**NARRATOR:**
> "Okay so it's not just spitting out a script. It's explaining what it's building, placing terrain, generating the moat water, placing castle walls — the whole thing. And now..."

---

## [0:38 – 0:52] SENT TO STUDIO

**SCREEN:** A green status badge pulses in the top-right corner of the ForjeGames editor:

> "Sent to Studio"

Cut immediately to Roblox Studio. The castle is there. Stone walls, a moat with water, a drawbridge. Camera slowly orbits around it.

**NARRATOR:**
> "That's actually insane. I typed eleven words and there's a full castle sitting in my Studio. I didn't write a single line of Luau. I didn't place one brick."

**SCREEN:** Playtest button. Character runs across the drawbridge. Splashes into the moat. Castle interior visible.

---

## [0:52 – 1:10] ITERATIVE BUILD — ADD NPCs

**SCREEN:** Switch back to ForjeGames editor. Type in the chat:

> `now add guards that patrol the walls`

AI responds instantly. New code streams. Cut to Studio — guard NPCs appear on the battlements, walking patrol routes. One turns and spots the player character.

**NARRATOR:**
> "And this is where it gets crazy. It's not one-shot. You just keep talking to it. Every message adds to what's already there. It remembers the castle. It knows where the walls are. The guards just... work."

---

## [1:10 – 1:28] PUSH FURTHER

**SCREEN:** Back in ForjeGames. Two more quick prompts, shown as fast cuts:

Prompt 1:
> `add a dungeon under the castle with torches and a locked door`

Cut to Studio — stone dungeon corridor, torch glow, iron door with a lock.

Prompt 2:
> `give the player a sword and a quest to find the castle key`

Cut to Studio — HUD appears with a quest tracker. Sword in player inventory. Key item on the floor of the dungeon.

**NARRATOR:**
> "Terrain. Lighting. Scripts. UI. Economy. It's building every layer at the same time, not just the visuals. This is a playable game loop right now."

---

## Production Notes

**Pacing:** Keep cuts tight. Every second of dead air kills retention for this audience. The Studio reveal at 0:38 is the money shot — linger for 3-4 seconds before cutting back.

**Music:** Lo-fi or synthwave, low in the mix. Nothing with drops or lyrics. Pull audio up slightly during the Studio reveals.

**Captions:** Auto-captions on. This audience watches with sound off on mobile.

**B-roll priority:** The "Sent to Studio" moment needs to land hard. Consider slow motion on the Studio viewport loading. That's the thing nobody has seen before.

**Call to action card:** Pin a comment with the link. Add it as an end screen. ForjeGames competes on zero-friction onboarding — the CTA needs to reflect that.
